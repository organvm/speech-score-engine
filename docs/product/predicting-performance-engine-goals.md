## Prediction

You want to build **a text-performance engine**: a system where language is treated not primarily as dialogue or literature, but as **score**.

More specifically, I think you are moving toward a form that sits between **short theatre, minimalist music, sound poetry, notation, and computational sequencing**. Not just “a play,” and not just “an audio adaptation,” but a machine or method that can generate and stage pieces where:

**text = rhythm = blocking = recurrence = transformation.**

## What in the photos points there

The WhatsApp exchange is the clearest signal. You are not reacting to the piece as ordinary dramatic writing. You react to it as a **timing problem**, a **coordination problem**, and an **audio-form problem**.

Your immediate question is not “what does it mean?” but effectively:
how is this performed, synchronized, counted, breathed, and heard?

That is decisive.

Then the Kindle photos show why. The piece stops behaving like conventional dramatic text and starts behaving like a **visual score**. The page becomes spatialized. Dialogue becomes pattern. Repetition becomes structure. Phrases split into atoms. Theatrical speech mutates into something closer to **phase composition** or **human sequencing**.

That combination strongly suggests you are interested in building a form where:

the page is readable as literature,
the performance is executable as score,
the audio is producible as rhythm,
and the whole thing can migrate between analog and digital embodiments.

## My strongest read

I think you want to build a **dramaturgical composition system for short-form works**.

A system that can produce pieces like this, but not merely imitate David Ives. Rather, it would generalize the underlying logic:

a scene starts in semantic clarity,
then repetition creates pressure,
pressure creates rhythm,
rhythm creates pattern,
pattern breaks language into units,
and units reorganize into a new event.

That is not standard playwriting. That is **linguistic choreography**.

## What the build probably is

At the highest level, I think the thing you want is a platform or practice with four simultaneous outputs:

### 1. A writing form
A script format where text is written as overlapping timed units, not just lines and cues.

### 2. A rehearsal/performance form
A way for performers to execute those units with count, tempo, entrances, overlap, interruption, recurrence, density, and release.

### 3. An audio form
A way to hear the piece as composition, possibly with click track, pulse, phase markers, stems, or voice-layer timing maps.

### 4. A visual/digital form
A display or interface where the script can be seen as score, animated as structure, or manipulated as a living arrangement of text blocks.

## In cleaner terms

I think you want to build:

**a score-native theatre system**
or
**a speech-music dramaturgy engine**
or
**a compositional environment for micro-plays built from repetition, variation, and phase.**

## My best sharper prediction

You want to build a contemporary performance system where:

a short textual premise can be expanded through repetition and permutation,
rendered as page, score, audio, and live event,
and possibly delivered across both physical and digital space.

In one sentence:

**You want to build an engine for transmuting language into rhythmic performance.**

A **software prototype** that lets you arrange lines as timed, repeating, overlapping speech blocks.
